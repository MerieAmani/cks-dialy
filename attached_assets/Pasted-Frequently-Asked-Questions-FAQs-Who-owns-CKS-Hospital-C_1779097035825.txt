Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who owns CKS Hospital?

CKS Kimuka Hospital is privately owned and managed by a team of experienced healthcare professionals committed to providing quality, affordable, and patient-centered healthcare services.

Are you open 24/7?

Yes. Our outpatient department, casualty, and emergency services are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Where are you located?

We are located along Ngong–Suswa Road at Kimuka Trading Center, next to Dominion Church in Kimuka.

What services do you offer?

We offer a wide range of healthcare services including:

* Outpatient services
* Emergency and casualty care
* Dialysis services
* Maternity services
* Laboratory services
* Radiology and ultrasound imaging
* Specialist clinics
* Daycare procedures
* Immunization and MCH services
* Ambulance evacuation and referral services

Do you have specialized clinics?

Yes. We offer specialist consultation clinics handled by qualified medical professionals in various fields.

Do you offer maternity services?

Yes. We provide maternity care services including antenatal care, delivery services, postnatal care, and maternal monitoring.

Do you accept SHA and other insurance?

Yes. We are SHA accredited and accept SHA services including:

* Civil Servants Scheme
* Mwalimu Scheme
* Primary Healthcare (PHC)

We also accept most major insurance providers.

Do I need to book in advance?

Walk-in patients are welcome. However, for specialist clinics and some procedures, advance booking is recommended to help minimize waiting time.

Is emergency care available 24/7?

Yes. Our emergency unit operates 24/7 for emergency treatment and stabilization before referrals or transfers when necessary. Ambulance evacuation services are also available.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept:

* Cash payments
* M-Pesa
* Debit/Credit cards
* Insurance payments
* SHA coverage
Do you offer dialysis services?

Yes. CKS Kimuka Hospital offers dialysis services managed by qualified healthcare professionals in a safe, comfortable, and patient-friendly environment.

What are the requirements for someone to start dialysis?

Before starting dialysis, a patient is usually required to:

* Be assessed by a doctor or nephrologist
* Have kidney function tests done
* Undergo laboratory investigations as advised
* Receive a recommendation for dialysis treatment based on their medical condition
* Have suitable dialysis access prepared, such as:
    * AV fistula
    * Catheter access (depending on urgency)

Patients are advised to carry:

* National ID or identification documents
* SHA card or insurance details (if applicable)
* Previous medical records and laboratory results
* Referral letter from their doctor, if available
Our medical team will guide patients and families through the assessment, preparation, and treatment process before dialysis begins.
